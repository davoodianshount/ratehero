The Core Concept
WHAT IS A
DSCR LOAN?
DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It's the single number that determines whether a rental property qualifies for financing — and it has nothing to do with your personal income, W-2s, or tax returns. The property pays for itself, and the loan qualifies on that basis alone.

DSCR Tiers — What the Number Means
1.25+
Strong Qualification
Access to best rates, highest LTV (up to 80%), and widest program selection. Most lenders prefer 1.25+ for optimal pricing.
1.00–1.24
Standard Qualification
Qualifies on most programs. Rate and LTV may be slightly adjusted. Still very workable with competitive pricing.
0.75–0.99
Sub-1.0 Programs
Specialized programs exist for sub-1.0 DSCR. Higher credit score requirements. Rate adjustments apply. Not available at all lenders.
Requirements
WHAT YOU
NEED TO QUALIFY.
DSCR loans have fewer requirements than conventional loans — but what they do require, they require consistently. Here's the complete picture.
📊
DSCR Ratio
≥ 0.75
Most programs require 0.75 minimum. 1.0+ is preferred. Higher DSCR = better rate and LTV. Calculated using market rent or existing lease, whichever the lender accepts.
💳
Credit Score
620+
Minimum 620 credit score. Best pricing at 720+. Foreign nationals may qualify with alternative credit documentation. No credit event programs available with some lenders.
🏠
Down Payment
20–25%
Typically 20% for standard DSCR. Some programs allow 15% with strong DSCR and credit. Cash-out refis go up to 75–80% LTV. STR properties may require 25%.
🏗️
Property Types
1–10 Units
Single family, 2–4 units, condos, townhomes, and 5–10 unit properties. STR and LTR both eligible. Non-warrantable condos case-by-case.
🏢
Entity / Vesting
LLC OK
DSCR loans can close in individual name, LLC, partnership, or trust. No personal income impact when vested in an entity. Great for portfolio protection.
📋
Documentation
No Tax Returns
No W-2, no tax returns, no employment verification. You'll need the lease or appraisal rent schedule, entity docs if applicable, and standard title/insurance docs.
