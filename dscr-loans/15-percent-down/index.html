DSCR Loans with 15% Down

85% LTV DSCR purchase loans for qualified investors. Get into your next property with less capital tied up.

Written by Sean Davoodian, NMLS #1252107 · Updated May 2026

DSCR loans with 15% down are available on a limited number of programs in 2026, capping out at 85% loan-to-value. Eligibility is tighter than 20–25% down DSCR loans: typical requirements include a 720+ FICO, a 1.10–1.25 minimum DSCR, a single-family or warrantable-condo subject property, and 6 months of reserves. Rate is usually 0.25–0.50% higher than the equivalent 75% LTV DSCR loan.

Who qualifies for 85% LTV DSCR

The 15% down programs are reserved for the strongest credit profile in DSCR lending. Most lenders require a minimum 720 FICO, with some pushing to 740 for the lowest available rate. The DSCR ratio threshold is also higher — typically 1.10 or 1.25 minimum, versus 1.0 on standard 75–80% LTV loans. The borrower must show a clean 24-month mortgage payment history (no late payments) and 6 months of PITIA reserves, double the 3-month reserve requirement on standard DSCR. A first-time investor with no prior rental history can still qualify, but some lenders apply a 0.125% pricing adjustment in that case.

Property and program restrictions

Eligible properties are typically limited to single-family homes and warrantable condos in primary metropolitan markets. 2–4 unit properties, condotels, non-warrantable condos, log homes, and rural properties are excluded from most 85% LTV DSCR programs. Loan amounts cap between $500,000 and $1.5 million depending on the lender and the market. Cash-out refinance is not available at 85% LTV — that LTV cap applies to purchase transactions only. Rate-and-term refinance maxes at 80% LTV on most programs that offer 85% on purchases. Property condition must be C3 or better on the appraisal report.

Cash-to-close math

On an $400,000 purchase with 15% down, the down payment is $60,000. Closing costs typically run 2–3% of the loan amount, adding $6,800–$10,200. Reserves of 6 months of PITIA on a $340,000 loan at a 7.5% rate plus taxes and insurance is roughly $16,000. Total cash-to-close including reserves: approximately $82,000–$86,000 on a $400,000 purchase. Compare to a 75% LTV purchase on the same property: $100,000 down plus $6,000 in closing costs plus $8,000 reserves = $114,000. The 85% LTV program reduces capital required by roughly 28%, with the trade-off of higher rate and tighter qualification.

When 15% down is the right choice

85% LTV DSCR makes sense when capital efficiency is the priority — when the investor wants to acquire more units faster and accepts a higher monthly payment in exchange for keeping more cash deployable for additional acquisitions or rehab capital. It also fits investors who can document strong reserves but don't want to drain liquidity. It does not fit deals where the property's DSCR is at or near 1.0, because the higher loan amount pushes the DSCR lower and most 85% programs require a 1.10+ DSCR floor. Always model the deal at both 75% and 85% LTV before committing — the rate differential can shift the breakeven calculation by year three or four.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a DSCR loan with 15% down?+
Yes, on select programs. 85% LTV DSCR purchase loans are available for borrowers with 720+ credit, 1.10+ DSCR, and 6 months of reserves on single-family or warrantable-condo properties.
What credit score is required for 85% LTV DSCR?+
Most 15% down DSCR programs require a minimum 720 FICO, with the best pricing tier starting at 740. A clean 24-month mortgage payment history is also typically required.
What's the rate difference between 75% and 85% LTV DSCR?+
Expect 0.25–0.50% higher rate on 85% LTV DSCR compared to 75% LTV on the same credit and DSCR profile. Pricing varies by lender and market conditions.
Can I do a cash-out refinance at 85% LTV on DSCR?+
No. 85% LTV DSCR is generally available on purchase transactions only. Cash-out refinances cap at 75% LTV on most programs. Rate-and-term refinances can reach 80–85% LTV depending on the lender.
What property types qualify for 85% LTV DSCR?+
Single-family homes and warrantable condos in primary metropolitan markets. 2–4 unit properties, non-warrantable condos, condotels, log homes, and rural properties are typically excluded from 85% LTV programs.
