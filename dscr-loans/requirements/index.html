DSCR Loan Requirements

What real lenders ask for in 2026 — credit, DSCR ratio, down payment, reserves, and property eligibility.

Written by Sean Davoodian, NMLS #1252107 · Updated May 2026

DSCR loan requirements in 2026 fall into five categories: a minimum credit score (usually 620–660), a debt service coverage ratio (typically 1.0 or higher), a down payment of 20–25%, 3–6 months of reserves, and a non-owner-occupied investment property. There is no income, employment, or tax-return requirement on most DSCR programs because the property's rental income carries the qualification.

Credit score and pricing tiers

Most DSCR programs accept credit scores starting at 620, with the strongest pricing reserved for borrowers above 720. A 660 minimum is typical for the more conservative wholesale lenders, while specialty lenders go down to 600 with offsetting compensating factors. Below 660, expect a higher rate, a lower maximum LTV (often capped at 70%), and additional reserve requirements. Above 740, you're in the best-pricing tier on virtually every DSCR program. Credit pulls for DSCR loans are tri-merge, and most lenders use the middle of the three FICO scores.

Debt service coverage ratio

DSCR is calculated by dividing the property's gross monthly rent by the total monthly mortgage payment, which includes principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and any HOA dues — collectively called PITIA. A DSCR of 1.0 means rent exactly covers the payment. A 1.25 DSCR is the threshold for the most aggressive pricing on most programs. Below 1.0, sub-ratio programs typically allow ratios down to 0.75, with a corresponding LTV reduction (usually capped at 70–75%). No-ratio DSCR programs waive the coverage requirement entirely and qualify on credit, reserves, and LTV alone, with rates roughly 0.25–0.50% higher than ratio-based pricing.

Down payment, LTV, and reserves

Standard DSCR purchase loans require 20–25% down, putting maximum LTV at 75–80%. A handful of programs offer 15% down (85% LTV) for strong-credit borrowers on single-family properties. Cash-out refinance LTV typically caps at 75%, and rate-and-term refinance can reach 80–85%. Reserves are calculated in months of PITIA — most programs require 3 months for primary investment loans and 6 months when the borrower owns multiple financed properties. Reserves can be held in checking, savings, or eligible retirement accounts (typically counted at 60–70% of stated value).

Property eligibility and rent documentation

Eligible properties include single-family homes, 2–4 unit residential, condos that pass project review, and townhomes. Most DSCR programs exclude rural properties, properties under 750 square feet, and condotels. Rural DSCR programs exist but with reduced LTV and tighter reserve requirements. For rent documentation, an existing lease covers leased properties; vacant properties qualify on a Form 1007 market rent appraisal completed by the appraiser. Short-term rental properties can use AirDNA projections, 12-month platform statements, or operating income records depending on the program.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need for a DSCR loan?+
Most DSCR programs require a minimum FICO of 620, with 660 being a more common floor at conservative wholesale lenders. The best pricing tier starts at 740. Borrowers between 600 and 619 may qualify on specialty programs at higher rates and lower LTV.
What is the minimum DSCR ratio to qualify?+
Standard DSCR programs require a minimum 1.0 ratio, meaning rent covers the full mortgage payment. Sub-1.0 programs allow ratios down to 0.75 with LTV typically capped at 70%. No-ratio DSCR programs waive the ratio requirement entirely.
How much down payment is required for a DSCR loan?+
Most DSCR purchase loans require 20–25% down. A few programs offer 15% down for strong-credit borrowers on single-family homes. Cash-out refinances are typically capped at 75% LTV and rate-and-term refis can reach 80–85% LTV.
Do DSCR loans require tax returns or W-2s?+
No. DSCR loans qualify entirely on the property's rental income relative to the mortgage payment. Tax returns, pay stubs, and W-2s are not required and not reviewed.
How many months of reserves are required?+
Most DSCR programs require 3 months of PITIA in reserves for the subject property, increasing to 6 months when the borrower carries multiple financed properties. Reserves can be in liquid accounts or eligible retirement accounts at a discounted value.
