How DSCR Works in Texas

The Property
Qualifies Itself

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio: Monthly Rent ÷ PITIA. If the property's rent covers the mortgage payment, it qualifies — regardless of your personal income, tax returns, or employment status.

Monthly Rent ÷ PITIA = DSCR
  • ✓DSCR 1.25+ — strongest programs, best rates
  • ✓DSCR 1.0–1.24 — qualifies on standard programs
  • ✓DSCR 0.75–0.99 — sub-1.0 programs available
  • ✓No-ratio options for the right borrower profile

Common Questions

Texas DSCR FAQ

Can I close a Texas DSCR loan in an LLC?+
Yes. LLC and entity borrowing is fully available on Texas DSCR loans. Texas is one of the most LLC-friendly states for real estate investors, and closing in an entity keeps investment debt off your personal credit.
Is Texas a good market for DSCR ratios?+
Yes — Texas consistently produces strong DSCR ratios because rent-to-price ratios remain favorable even in major metros. Secondary cities like San Antonio, Lubbock, and El Paso often produce DSCR ratios of 1.3 or higher on modest down payments.
Does Rate Hero lend on Texas multifamily properties?+
Yes. We offer DSCR loans on 1–4 unit properties statewide, and select 5–10 unit programs for experienced investors. Multifamily properties in Texas's major metros are among our most active deal types.
Can I close a Texas DSCR loan in an LLC?+
Yes. LLC and entity borrowing is available on all DSCR programs in Texas. Closing in an LLC keeps investment debt off your personal credit profile and provides liability protection across your portfolio. We close a significant percentage of Texas deals in LLCs and limited partnerships.
How fast can Rate Hero close a DSCR loan in Texas?+
Our average close time on Texas DSCR loans is 21 days. Purchase transactions requiring an appraisal may add a few days depending on appraiser availability in your specific market. Rate-and-term refinances with existing leases in place can sometimes close in 14 days.
