DSCR Loans for LLCs

Close in your LLC, partnership, or corporation. Keep investment debt off your personal credit profile and protect personal assets.

Written by Sean Davoodian, NMLS #1252107 · Updated May 2026

DSCR loans can be closed in the name of an LLC, limited partnership, S-corporation, or other business entity. The entity becomes the borrower, the property is titled to the entity, and the loan does not appear on the principal's personal credit report. The trade-off is a personal guaranty from each member with 25%+ ownership and a slightly more involved closing process.

Why investors close in an LLC

There are three reasons real estate investors close DSCR loans in an LLC. First, liability protection — a properly maintained LLC limits the investor's personal exposure to claims arising from the property, such as tenant injury or environmental liability. Second, credit profile management — entity-level debt does not report on personal credit, which preserves personal DTI capacity for primary residence purchases or other consumer financing. Third, partnership structuring — multiple investors can hold ownership shares in a single LLC that owns multiple properties, simplifying capital partner arrangements. The asset protection benefit assumes the LLC is properly capitalized, maintains its own bank account, and follows corporate formalities.

Eligible entity structures

DSCR lenders accept a range of entity structures: single-member LLCs, multi-member LLCs, limited partnerships, general partnerships, S-corporations, and revocable trusts (less common). Series LLCs are accepted by some lenders but require additional documentation. Foreign-domiciled LLCs are eligible but require an in-state registered agent and may need a foreign qualification filing in the property's state. The entity must be in good standing — current on annual filings and franchise taxes. Newly formed LLCs are accepted; there is no seasoning requirement on the entity itself.

Personal guaranty and credit qualification

Even with an LLC closing, the lender still pulls personal credit on every member who owns 25% or more of the entity. A personal guaranty is required from each guarantor. Credit qualification standards mirror personal-name DSCR loans: 620–660 minimum credit score, with the best pricing at 740+. The personal guaranty means a default on the LLC loan can still impact the guarantor's credit — but the loan itself does not appear as an open trade line on the personal report unless the LLC defaults. For multi-member LLCs, the guarantor with the strongest credit profile typically anchors the qualification.

Closing in an LLC: documents and timeline

Closing in an LLC adds 2–4 documents to the standard DSCR loan package. You'll need the articles of organization (or certificate of formation), the operating agreement, a certificate of good standing from the state of formation, the EIN assignment letter from the IRS, and a borrower resolution authorizing the loan and identifying the signing officer. If you're titling the property in the LLC for the first time at closing, the title company will prepare a deed transfer at the same closing. Timeline impact is minimal — one to two days of additional underwriting review. Total closing time on an LLC DSCR loan is typically 21–25 days, comparable to personal-name closings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a DSCR loan in the name of my LLC?+
Yes. DSCR loans are commonly closed in the name of an LLC, limited partnership, S-corporation, or other business entity. The entity becomes the borrower and the property is titled to the entity.
Does the LLC loan show up on my personal credit?+
No, not as an open trade line. The loan is reported under the entity's tax ID, not your Social Security number. Personal credit is pulled at qualification because of the personal guaranty, but the active loan does not consume personal DTI capacity.
Do I need to personally guarantee an LLC DSCR loan?+
Yes. Every member with 25% or more ownership signs a personal guaranty. The guaranty makes the principals liable for the loan in the event of LLC default, even though the loan is in the entity's name.
Can I form a brand-new LLC to close a DSCR loan?+
Yes. There is no entity seasoning requirement. You can form an LLC, obtain an EIN, and use it to close a DSCR loan within the same month. The entity must be in good standing and have its operating agreement in place at closing.
Are DSCR loan rates higher when closed in an LLC?+
No. Most DSCR lenders price LLC closings at the same rate as personal-name closings on the same credit and DSCR profile. A small number of lenders charge an entity-closing fee of 0.125–0.25 points, but rate sheets are otherwise identical.
