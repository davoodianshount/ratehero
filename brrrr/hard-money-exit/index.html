Hard Money Exit

Hard Money Exit Loans

Refinance hard money or bridge debt into 30-year DSCR financing. Cash-out the rehab equity. 21-day close.

Written by Sean Davoodian, NMLS #1252107 · Updated May 2026

A hard money exit refinance replaces short-term rehab debt with a long-term DSCR loan once the property is stabilized. The most common use case is the BRRRR strategy: buy with hard money, rehab, rent, then refinance into a 30-year DSCR loan that pays off the hard money and recycles the equity for the next deal. Typical exit timeline is 6–9 months from purchase to refinance funding, including 3–6 months of seasoning and a 21-day refinance close.

Timing the exit refinance

Three milestones determine when you can refinance out of hard money: rehab completion, lease-up, and seasoning. Rehab completion is verified by the appraiser at the new appraisal — the property must be in C3 or better condition with the work complete and permits closed. Lease-up means the property is rented and the lease is signed, or the appraiser completes a Form 1007 market rent if the property remains vacant. Seasoning is the title-ownership window required by the new lender — most DSCR programs require 6 months of title seasoning, with a handful allowing 3–4 months at reduced LTV. Plan the rehab and lease-up timeline so the property is ready when the seasoning window opens.

Cash-out math on a BRRRR exit

The math determines whether you've successfully recycled your capital. Example: purchase price $180,000 with $45,000 in hard money points and rehab carry, plus $80,000 in rehab cost = $305,000 all-in. After repair value (ARV) appraises at $400,000. A 75% LTV cash-out refinance produces a $300,000 new loan. After paying off the $260,000 hard money balance, $5,000 in closing costs, and any prepayment penalty on the hard money, you net roughly $35,000 in cash plus a stabilized rental property with $100,000 in retained equity. The deal worked if the new DSCR loan's payment plus expenses cash flows positive on the lease rent.

Hard money payoff and prepayment penalties

Hard money loans carry their own prepayment terms that affect the exit calculus. Most hard money lenders structure a 6-month or 12-month minimum interest period — meaning even if you refinance at month 4, you owe interest through month 6. Some carry an explicit prepayment penalty of 1–3% of the loan balance for early payoff. Always confirm the hard money exit terms before signing — a 6-month minimum interest charge on a $250,000 loan at 11% adds roughly $13,750 to the exit cost. The DSCR refinance lender pays off the hard money lender at closing via the title company; you don't need to fund the payoff out of pocket.

DSCR exit eligibility and pitfalls

Three things commonly trip up a hard money exit. First, the appraised value comes in below the ARV underwritten by the hard money lender — the BRRRR math fails when actual appraisal is 10–15% below pro-forma. Second, the property doesn't qualify on DSCR at the targeted loan amount because rents came in below pro-forma; sub-1.0 or no-ratio programs solve this with reduced LTV. Third, the rehab work isn't permit-closed at the time of the refinance appraisal — open permits trigger appraiser callouts that delay funding. Get the new appraisal ordered as soon as the property is stabilized; identify any permit gaps early; and have a sub-1.0 backup program identified before you submit, in case the rent or DSCR comes in light.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I need to wait before refinancing out of hard money?+
Most DSCR programs require 6 months of title seasoning. A handful of programs allow 3–4 month seasoning at reduced LTV (typically 70%). Delayed-financing exceptions allow refinance within 6 months if certain conditions are met.
Can I do a cash-out refinance on a BRRRR property?+
Yes. DSCR cash-out refinance is the standard BRRRR exit. Maximum LTV is typically 75% of the new appraised value (ARV). Proceeds pay off the hard money loan and return remaining equity to the borrower.
Do I need rental history to refinance out of hard money?+
No. The DSCR refinance can qualify on a Form 1007 market rent appraisal if the property is vacant, or on a signed lease if the property is leased. Rental history on the property itself is not required.
What if the appraisal comes in below my expected ARV?+
If the appraisal is 5–15% below your projected ARV, the new loan amount drops proportionally. Sub-1.0 or no-ratio DSCR programs can sometimes preserve the loan amount at lower LTV. You may also need to bring additional cash to close to make the BRRRR math work.
How fast can a hard money exit refinance close?+
Typical close time is 21 days from full submission to funding. The appraisal turn-time and title seasoning verification are usually the critical-path items. Rate-and-term refinances on existing leases can close as fast as 14 days.
