Section 01 · Introduction
WHY STR
FINANCING
IS DIFFERENT
Short-term rental income is real, documented, and often dramatically higher than long-term rent — but conventional lenders don't know how to evaluate it. They look at your tax return, see a rental property with aggressive depreciation and expenses, and reject the loan.
DSCR programs for STR were built specifically for this scenario. The lender qualifies the property on its projected or actual short-term rental income — not your personal tax return, not your W-2, not your Schedule E.

"The STR that generates $6,000/month on Airbnb can look like a $0 income property on a tax return. DSCR fixes that."

02
Income Documentation
THE 3 WAYS
TO PROVE
STR INCOME
Why This Matters
Lenders can't put "probably rents for $5K/month on Airbnb" on a loan. They need a documented, third-party verified income figure. Three approved methods exist — and when multiple sources are submitted, the lowest figure is used to qualify.
The Three Methods
📊
Method 1 — AirDNA Rentalizer
For purchase transactions where no rental history exists. The AirDNA Property Earning Potential Report projects 12-month gross revenue. Requirements: market score 60 or higher, minimum 3 comparable properties, forecast dated within 90 days. A 20% vacancy factor is applied unless the appraiser documents a specific occupancy rate.
📋
Method 2 — 12-Month Platform History
The Airbnb, VRBO, or property management platform provides a 12-month statement identifying the property, showing rents collected, and itemizing all vendor or management fees. Qualifying income is net of all fees. Primary method for refinance transactions.
🏦
Method 3 — Bank Statement Deposits
12 months of bank statements showing STR deposits, supported by rental records for the property. When used alongside AirDNA or platform history, the lowest of all income figures is the qualifying number.
📌 Key Rule
"When you submit multiple income sources, the lender always uses the lowest figure. Don't volunteer extra documentation that might lower your qualifying income — work with your advisor on which single method produces the best result."
03
DSCR STR Loan Structure
HOW DSCR
STR LOANS
QUALIFY
The Formula
DSCR = Net Qualifying STR Income ÷ PITIA
The net qualifying income is the documented STR income after the vacancy factor is applied. A DSCR of 1.0 means rent exactly covers the payment. Above 1.0 means positive cash flow from the lender's perspective.
Program Parameters
📈
DSCR Thresholds
1.25+ Strong — best rates, highest LTV. 1.0–1.24 Standard — qualifies at full program. 0.75–0.99 Sub-1.0 programs available. Below 0.75 No-ratio option up to 80% LTV.
🏠
Property Types
SFR, 2–4 units, and condominiums (must verify HOA allows STR). 5–10 unit multifamily not typically eligible for STR qualification. Condotels excluded.
⚠️
Vacancy Factor
A default 20% vacancy factor is applied to STR income unless the appraiser provides a specific occupancy rate. AirDNA projections also use revenue = daily rate × occupancy rate from the report.
🔍
HOA Due Diligence
Always review CC&Rs and HOA rules before applying. A condo or community that prohibits short-term rentals disqualifies the STR loan at the property level — no lender can override the HOA.
📋 Example
"Mountain cabin purchase. AirDNA market score 78, $5,200/mo gross. After 20% vacancy: $4,160. PITIA $2,950. DSCR 1.41 — strong approval, best program rates."

Section 08
STR FINANCING
MISTAKES
TO AVOID
📋
Submitting multiple income sources without thinking
When you submit AirDNA AND a platform statement AND bank deposits, the lender uses the lowest. If your AirDNA projection is lower than your actual history, don't volunteer AirDNA — it will reduce your qualifying income and potentially kill the deal.
🏢
Not checking HOA rules before applying
A condo with STR-prohibiting CC&Rs cannot be financed as a DSCR STR loan regardless of what the lender allows. This is a hard stop that costs you the appraisal fee if discovered late. Always review HOA governing documents before ordering an appraisal.
📊
Using an AirDNA market with a score below 60
AirDNA projections are only acceptable when the market score is 60 or higher. A beautiful property in a thin vacation rental market with a score of 52 cannot use AirDNA for qualification — you'll need 12 months of actual rental history instead.
🔢
Forgetting the 20% vacancy factor in the math
If AirDNA projects $6,000/month, you qualify on $4,800/month — not $6,000. Investors who run their DSCR math on gross projections and then discover the vacancy factor often find a deal they thought penciled at 1.25 actually comes in at 0.99.
⏰
Letting the AirDNA report go stale
The AirDNA Rentalizer report must be dated within 90 days of the note date. If your closing gets delayed — an extended inspection period, title issues, lender backlog — you may need a fresh AirDNA report. Budget for this in your timeline.
💰
Treating all STR cash-out refis like LTR cash-out refis
STR cash-out refinances typically have a lower maximum LTV than LTR cash-out refis. A standard DSCR cash-out might go to 75% LTV on a long-term rental but only 70–72% on an STR. Run the numbers with STR-specific LTV caps before structuring the transaction.
