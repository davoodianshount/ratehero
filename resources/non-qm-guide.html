Income Qualification Paths
FOUR WAYS
WE QUALIFY YOU.
No W-2? No tax return? That's fine. Here are the four paths we use to document your income for a Non-QM loan — and the documents each one requires.
🏦
Bank Statement Method
Best for: Self-Employed / Business Owners
We average 12–24 months of your personal or business bank deposits to establish qualifying income. Business accounts use an expense factor (typically 50%) to calculate net income.
What we need:
24 months personal OR business bank statements · Business license or CPA letter confirming 2+ years self-employed
📊
P&L Only Method
Best for: Complex deduction situations
A CPA-prepared profit & loss statement for the last 12–24 months. No bank statements, no tax returns. The P&L must be prepared by a licensed CPA or enrolled agent.
What we need:
12–24 month P&L prepared by CPA · Business license · CPA attestation letter
📋
1099 Income Method
Best for: Independent contractors, 1099 earners
We use your 1099 forms as the income basis — without requiring full tax returns. The gross 1099 amount is used, not the net after deductions. Typically requires 2 years of 1099s.
What we need:
2 years 1099 forms · Recent bank statements (2–3 months) · Basic employment/contract confirmation
💎
Asset Depletion Method
Best for: Retired, high net worth, or income-light
We divide your liquid assets by the loan term (months) to establish a monthly qualifying income. Example: $1.5M in assets ÷ 360 months = $4,167/month qualifying income.
What we need:
3 months statements for all qualifying accounts · Vesting documentation · No withdrawal penalty confirmation
