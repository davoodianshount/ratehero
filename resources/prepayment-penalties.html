The Basics

What Is a Prepayment Penalty?

A prepayment penalty is a fee charged when you pay off your loan — or a large chunk of it — before the penalty period ends. On business-purpose and investor loans like DSCR, this is standard. It's not a punishment — it's a lender protection.

Lenders price DSCR loans assuming they'll earn interest for a certain period. If you sell or refinance in year 2, they've lost 28 years of expected interest. The prepayment penalty compensates for that.

The penalty period typically runs 3, 5, or sometimes 7 years from origination. After that, you can sell or refinance freely with no fee. The difference between $0 and $15,000 can come down to closing just six days early.

Important Distinction

Prepayment penalties on business-purpose loans (DSCR, investment property) are legal in all 50 states. Owner-occupied residential restrictions under federal QM rules do not apply. DSCR loans are almost always business-purpose — which is why these penalties are routine.

Extra Payments & Partial Paydown

Can You Make Extra Payments?

Not all penalties block every extra dollar. Here's what's typically allowed — and what triggers the fee.

Generally Allowed

These typically do not trigger a penalty — always confirm in your loan documents.

  • Regular monthly P&I payments
  • Extra principal up to 20%/yr on 6-month programs
  • Escrow catch-up payments
  • Insurance claim proceeds for repairs
  • Any payment after penalty period ends

Triggers the Penalty

These actions typically trigger a full prepayment penalty calculation.

  • Full payoff via sale or refinance
  • Refinancing to a new loan
  • Lump-sum paydowns exceeding allowed threshold
  • Title transfer triggering due-on-sale clause

6-Month Interest — Extra Payments Often Allowed

Many 6-month interest structures allow you to pay down up to 20% of the original principal per year without triggering the penalty. This is a meaningful advantage for BRRRR investors building equity fast. Always confirm the exact threshold in your Note or Deed of Trust.
