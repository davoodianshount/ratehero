Section 04
FIRST-TIME INVESTOR
MISTAKES TO AVOID
📱
Going to your personal bank first
Your bank doesn't offer DSCR loans and may not have investment property programs at all. They'll underwrite it as a conventional loan against your personal income — which often kills the deal before it starts.
📊
Forgetting to include all PITIA costs
The DSCR calculation uses Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance, AND HOA. Investors who run a quick mortgage estimate and forget property taxes, insurance, or HOA often find their DSCR looks better than it actually is.
🏢
Waiting to set up the LLC
If you want to buy in an LLC, set it up before going under contract — not after. Changing vesting after rate lock can cause issues. Get your entity structure sorted before you make offers.
💳
Using credit cards to fund the down payment
Lenders verify that your down payment funds have been in your account for 60+ days ("seasoned"). Last-minute credit card advances or transfers from unknown sources can blow up your approval.
📋
Not accounting for reserves requirement
Most investment property loans require 3–6 months of PITIA in reserves after closing. Investors who spend every dollar on the down payment and closing costs often get surprised by the reserves check at closing.
⏰
Waiting until you're under contract to call a lender
Knowing your loan program, rate range, and down payment before you start shopping changes everything. You make better offers, negotiate from strength, and never lose a deal to a financing contingency.
