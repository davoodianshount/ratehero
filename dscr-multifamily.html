Can You Get a DSCR Loan on a Vacant Property? Yes — Here's How

SD
Sean Davoodian
NMLS # 1252107 · March 24, 2026 · 5 min read

One of the most common questions from DSCR borrowers: what if the property has no tenant at application? DSCR programs explicitly accommodate unleased properties — you are not required to have a signed lease in hand to qualify.

The Unleased Unit Rule

For unleased 1–4 unit properties, 100% vacancy is permitted under DSCR guidelines. The gross rents shown on the appraiser's Form 1007/1025 market rent analysis are used for qualification with no vacancy factor applied. The appraiser's opinion of market rent — not an actual lease — is the qualifying income source.

Bringing in a New Tenant

If you execute a lease before closing, you can use that new lease to qualify — up to 120% higher than the 1007 market rent. To use a lease that exceeds the 1007 by more than 20%, you must document that the security deposit and first month's rent have been collected before loan closing. This protects against manufactured high leases with no actual tenant commitment.

Why This Matters for BRRRR Investors

BRRRR investors who complete a rehab and list the property for rent — but haven't placed a tenant yet — can still close the DSCR exit refinance. The appraiser establishes market rent based on comparable rentals in the area, and that income supports the DSCR qualification. You're not stuck waiting for a tenant to start your exit.

Maximize Your Qualifying Rent

Property condition directly affects the appraiser's market rent analysis. A freshly renovated, well-maintained unit commands higher comparable rents than a dated one. The appraisal is where your rehab investment translates directly into higher qualifying income — invest in presentation before the appraisal is scheduled.
