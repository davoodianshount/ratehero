How DSCR Works in Ohio

The Property
Qualifies Itself

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio: Monthly Rent ÷ PITIA. If the property's rent covers the mortgage payment, it qualifies — regardless of your personal income, tax returns, or employment status.

Monthly Rent ÷ PITIA = DSCR
  • ✓DSCR 1.25+ — strongest programs, best rates
  • ✓DSCR 1.0–1.24 — qualifies on standard programs
  • ✓DSCR 0.75–0.99 — sub-1.0 programs available
  • ✓No-ratio options for the right borrower profile

Common Questions

Ohio DSCR FAQ

What are the best Ohio cities for DSCR loans?+
Cleveland, Columbus, and Cincinnati are our three highest-volume Ohio markets. Cleveland produces the best raw DSCR ratios due to very low purchase prices. Columbus has the strongest long-term appreciation. Cincinnati offers consistent demand across all property types.
Does Rate Hero lend on Cleveland turnkey properties?+
Yes. Cleveland turnkey properties are among the most popular DSCR loan scenarios we close in Ohio. Many Cleveland investment properties produce DSCR ratios of 1.4–1.6 on modest purchase prices. We work with both individual investors and turnkey operators.
Can I get a DSCR loan on an Ohio 2-4 unit property?+
Yes. 2–4 unit properties are fully eligible for DSCR loans in Ohio. The qualifying income is typically the combined rent from all occupied units, or the Form 1007 market rent for vacant units. Duplexes and triplexes in Columbus and Cleveland are some of our most common Ohio deal types.
Can I close a Ohio DSCR loan in an LLC?+
Yes. LLC and entity borrowing is available on all DSCR programs in Ohio. Closing in an LLC keeps investment debt off your personal credit profile and provides liability protection across your portfolio. We close a significant percentage of Ohio deals in LLCs and limited partnerships.
How fast can Rate Hero close a DSCR loan in Ohio?+
Our average close time on Ohio DSCR loans is 21 days. Purchase transactions requiring an appraisal may add a few days depending on appraiser availability in your specific market. Rate-and-term refinances with existing leases in place can sometimes close in 14 days.
