DSCR Loans for 5-10 Unit Multifamily: What Investors Need to Know

SD
our team
NMLS # 1252107 · February 11, 2026 · 5 min read

Investors scaling beyond single-family and small multifamily eventually discover that 5-10 unit properties fall in an awkward gap — too small for commercial lending, but too large for standard residential. DSCR programs bridge this gap with investor-friendly qualification and no personal income requirements.

How 5-10 Unit DSCR Works

The same DSCR formula applies: Gross Rental Income ÷ PITIA. For a 5-unit building generating $8,500/month with a $6,200 PITIA, DSCR = 1.37. This qualifies under most 5-10 unit DSCR programs without any personal income documentation.

Borrower Experience Requirements

Unlike 1-4 unit DSCR loans which allow first-time investors, 5-10 unit multifamily programs typically require borrower experience. Documented ownership and management history of at least one rental property is generally required. Working in the property management industry also satisfies the experience requirement.

Eligible Property Conditions

The property must meet minimum condition standards. Lenders want properties that are stabilized or near-stabilized — significant deferred maintenance or properties requiring major rehabilitation may not qualify without additional conditions or holdbacks.

Appraisal Requirements

5-10 unit properties require specific appraisal attachments documenting the income approach to value. The appraiser must complete a rent roll, expense analysis, and income capitalization approach. This is a more involved appraisal than a standard 1-4 unit residential report.

LLC Lending for Multifamily

LLC and entity borrowing is fully available for 5-10 unit DSCR loans. Most sophisticated multifamily investors close in entity names for liability protection. The same entity documentation requirements apply as for 1-4 unit DSCR LLC loans.
