Stand-Alone CES: The Best Way to Access Equity Without Refinancing Your First

SD
Sean Davoodian
NMLS # 1252107 · April 13, 2026 · 5 min read

For the millions of homeowners with first mortgages locked in at 3–4%, a cash-out refinance means replacing a historically low rate on the entire balance. A stand-alone closed-end second is almost always the better strategy — preserving the first mortgage while accessing equity through a subordinate second lien.

The Rate Preservation Math

If your first mortgage is $420,000 at 3.25% and you need $100,000 in equity access: a cash-out refinance at 7.5% increases your monthly payment on the existing balance by roughly $1,800/month. A stand-alone $100,000 CES at 10% adds approximately $1,320/month — on only the $100,000 second lien, not the full balance. The stand-alone approach costs significantly less despite the higher CES rate.

Total Interest Cost Over Time

Don't just compare monthly payments — compare total interest cost over the remaining loan life. The first mortgage at 3.25% generates far less interest over 20 years than the same balance refinanced at 7.5%. Even a CES at a premium rate costs less in total interest than losing the low rate on the full balance. Your loan advisor should provide a full 10-year cost comparison before you decide.

Stand-Alone Closing Costs

A stand-alone CES closing costs $1,500–3,500 in typical market conditions versus $4,000–10,000+ for a first lien cash-out refinance. The lower transaction cost improves the economics further — particularly for equity draws under $100,000 where closing costs represent a meaningful percentage of proceeds.

Property Listing Seasoning

Some stand-alone CES programs restrict cash-out transactions if the property has been listed for sale on the MLS within the past 6 months. If you've had the property listed and withdrawn the listing, provide documentation of the cancellation date and confirm the program's seasoning requirement is satisfied before proceeding.
