FHA loans have helped millions of Americans achieve homeownership — particularly first-time buyers and those with less-than-perfect credit. This guide covers fha vs usda loan — which is better for rural properties? with current 2026 guidelines.

What Is an FHA Loan?

FHA loans are insured by the Federal Housing Administration, which allows lenders to offer more flexible terms than conventional programs. The lower credit score requirements and 3.5% down payment option make FHA a popular choice for first-time homebuyers and borrowers rebuilding credit.

FHA Requirements in 2026

Current FHA guidelines require:

  • Credit score: 580+ for 3.5% down; 500–579 for 10% down
  • Down payment: 3.5% minimum (580+ credit)
  • DTI ratio: Up to 57% with compensating factors
  • MIP: Required for the life of the loan (if less than 10% down)

FHA vs Conventional — When FHA Wins

FHA makes the most sense when your credit score is between 580–679 or your down payment is limited to 3.5%. Above 680 credit and 5%+ down, conventional often provides better terms. Rate Hero will run both scenarios and show you exactly which program saves you more money.
