Investment Property HELOC

Pull a line of credit against your rental property's equity. LLC vesting available. Up to 80% combined LTV on second-lien programs.

Written by Sean Davoodian, NMLS #1252107 · Updated May 2026

An investment property HELOC is a second-lien revolving line of credit secured by a non-owner-occupied rental property. The HELOC sits behind the existing first mortgage and lets the investor draw funds for acquisitions, rehab, debt consolidation, or reserves without refinancing the first lien. Maximum combined LTV (first mortgage + HELOC limit) is typically 75–80% on investor properties, with rates usually 1–2% higher than primary-residence HELOCs.

How investment property HELOCs differ from primary HELOCs

Three differences matter. First, combined LTV is lower — investor HELOCs typically cap at 75–80% combined LTV, versus 85–90% on primary residences. Second, rates are higher — expect a 1–2% premium over equivalent primary-residence HELOC pricing, reflecting the second-lien risk on a non-owner-occupied property. Third, qualification is tighter — most lenders require 680+ FICO for investment HELOCs (versus 660+ for primary), and DTI calculations include the full primary residence mortgage plus all rental property debt. A few specialty lenders qualify investment HELOCs on rental cash flow rather than personal income, which works well for self-employed borrowers.

Draw period, repayment, and rate structure

Most investment property HELOCs have a 10-year draw period followed by a 15- or 20-year repayment period — the same structure as primary-residence HELOCs. During the draw period, the borrower can draw and repay against the line repeatedly; minimum monthly payments are typically interest-only. After the draw period closes, the line converts to amortizing principal-and-interest payments on the outstanding balance. Rates are variable on most programs, indexed to Prime plus a margin (often Prime + 1.0% to Prime + 3.0% depending on credit and CLTV). A handful of lenders offer fixed-rate HELOC products or fixed-rate advance options inside an otherwise variable line.

LLC vesting and entity-level HELOCs

Investment property HELOCs can be closed in the name of an LLC on a smaller subset of programs than full DSCR loans. Most major HELOC lenders only originate in personal name, even when the underlying first mortgage is LLC-titled. Specialty non-QM lenders offer entity-level HELOCs with similar requirements to entity DSCR loans: personal guaranty from each 25%+ member, certificate of good standing, operating agreement, and EIN documentation. Entity HELOCs typically price 0.25–0.50% higher than personal-name HELOCs and have stricter combined LTV limits. If LLC vesting is required, work with a non-QM-focused broker rather than a retail bank — most retail banks decline entity HELOCs entirely.

Common uses for an investment property HELOC

Five use cases drive most investment property HELOC volume. Acquisition down payments — the HELOC funds the down payment on a new rental, recycled into long-term DSCR financing once the new property is stabilized. Rehab capital — funding a value-add renovation on an existing rental without refinancing the first lien. Hard money payoff — paying off short-term rehab debt while waiting for seasoning to clear on a refinance. Reserves cushion — establishing a draw line to cover unexpected vacancies or repairs without forced sales. Bridge between deals — using the HELOC to close on the next acquisition while the prior deal's refinance is still in process. The flexibility of a revolving line is the structural advantage over a one-time cash-out refinance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a HELOC on a rental property?+
Yes. Investment property HELOCs are available from a smaller pool of lenders than primary-residence HELOCs, typically capped at 75–80% combined LTV with rates 1–2% higher than primary HELOC pricing.
What's the maximum combined LTV on an investment HELOC?+
Most programs cap combined LTV (first mortgage + HELOC limit) at 75–80% on investor properties. A few specialty programs reach 85% with strong credit and reserves.
Can I get an investment property HELOC in an LLC?+
On a smaller subset of programs, yes. Most retail banks decline entity HELOCs entirely. Specialty non-QM lenders offer LLC-vested investment HELOCs with personal guaranty and the same entity documentation as DSCR loans.
Are investment property HELOC rates fixed or variable?+
Variable on most programs, indexed to Prime plus a margin (Prime + 1.0% to Prime + 3.0% typical). Some lenders offer fixed-rate HELOC products or the option to fix individual draws inside an otherwise variable line.
Can I qualify for an investment HELOC on rental cash flow instead of personal income?+
On specialty programs, yes. A few non-QM HELOC programs qualify on the property's rental income relative to combined debt service, similar to a DSCR loan. These programs are useful for self-employed borrowers whose tax returns understate cash flow.
