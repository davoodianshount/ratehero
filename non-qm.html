Common Questions

Hard Money Exit FAQ

When is the right time to exit hard money?+

As soon as the property is stabilized and rented. You need a tenant in place (or a strong market rent analysis) so we can calculate your DSCR. The sooner you exit, the sooner you stop paying double-digit interest.

What if the property was recently renovated and is newly rented?+

That's the ideal scenario for a hard money exit. As long as you have a signed lease or we can establish market rent via appraisal, we can proceed. There's no seasoning requirement on how long the tenant has been in place for most programs.

Can I take cash out when I exit?+

Yes. If the property has appreciated or you've built equity through the rehab, a cash-out refinance on exit is a powerful move — you repay the hard money loan and pull out equity to fund your next acquisition, all in one transaction.

Do I need to show personal income to qualify?+

No. This is a DSCR loan — qualification is based entirely on the property's rental income. No W-2s, no tax returns, no personal income verification of any kind.

What's the minimum DSCR to qualify?+

Most programs require a DSCR of 1.0 — meaning rent covers the mortgage payment. We have programs down to 0.75 for strong profiles. If your DSCR is tight, we'll tell you upfront and discuss options before you spend time on an application.
