How DSCR Works in Kansas

The Property
Qualifies Itself

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio: Monthly Rent ÷ PITIA. If the property's rent covers the mortgage payment, it qualifies — regardless of your personal income, tax returns, or employment status.

Monthly Rent ÷ PITIA = DSCR
  • ✓DSCR 1.25+ — strongest programs, best rates
  • ✓DSCR 1.0–1.24 — qualifies on standard programs
  • ✓DSCR 0.75–0.99 — sub-1.0 programs available
  • ✓No-ratio options for the right borrower profile
Qualification Requirements

No W-2.
No Tax Returns.
No Problem.

DSCR loans evaluate the property — not the borrower's income history. What you need:

Common Questions

Kansas DSCR FAQ

Is Wichita a good market for DSCR investors?+
Yes. Wichita is one of the most affordable large cities in the country and consistently produces strong DSCR ratios. The city's aerospace industry and Wichita State University create stable rental demand, and properties routinely achieve 1.35–1.5 DSCR on standard down payments.
Can I get a DSCR loan near Kansas City in Kansas?+
Yes. Overland Park, Olathe, and Kansas City KS all qualify for our DSCR programs. These Kansas suburbs benefit from the KC metro's growth while carrying lower property tax rates than Missouri-side properties.
Does Rate Hero lend in Lawrence for student rental properties?+
Yes. Lawrence is a strong student rental market anchored by the University of Kansas. Properties near campus consistently produce favorable DSCR ratios. We lend throughout Lawrence and the surrounding Douglas County area.
Can I close a Kansas DSCR loan in an LLC?+
Yes. LLC and entity borrowing is available on all DSCR programs in Kansas. Closing in an LLC keeps investment debt off your personal credit profile and provides liability protection across your portfolio. We close a significant percentage of Kansas deals in LLCs and limited partnerships.
How fast can Rate Hero close a DSCR loan in Kansas?+
Our average close time on Kansas DSCR loans is 21 days. Purchase transactions requiring an appraisal may add a few days depending on appraiser availability in your specific market. Rate-and-term refinances with existing leases in place can sometimes close in 14 days.
