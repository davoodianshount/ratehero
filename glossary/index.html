Real Estate Investor Glossary

Plain English. Real Definitions.

Definitions for the loan and investment terms that matter to investors. Written by Sean Davoodian of Rate Hero, Inc., a licensed mortgage company (NMLS #2822806).

DSCR Programs

DSCR Programs

DSCR
The ratio of property income to debt service — qualifies your loan.
No-Ratio DSCR
Qualifies on credit, reserves, and LTV — no rental coverage required.
LTV
How much you're borrowing relative to property value.
BRRRR
Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat — the investor scaling strategy.
LTC
Loan amount as a percentage of total project cost.
Sub-1.0 DSCR
DSCR programs that qualify properties below 1.0 ratio.
Loan Products

Loan Products

Non-QM
Loans that don't meet QM rules — alternative income docs accepted.
Bridge Loan
Short-term loan that bridges to permanent financing.
HELOC
Revolving credit secured by your home's equity.
Hard Money
Short-term, asset-based real estate financing.
Conventional Loan
Mortgage conforming to Fannie/Freddie standards.
FHA Loan
Federal-insured loan with low down payment.
VA Loan
Veterans Affairs guaranteed mortgage with $0 down.
Jumbo Loan
Mortgage exceeding conforming loan limits.
Appraisal
Independent property valuation that caps how much a lender will lend.
Underwriting
The lender's process for approving, denying, or conditioning your loan.
Prepayment Penalty
Fee for paying off your loan early — common on DSCR loans.
PMI
Private Mortgage Insurance — required on conventional loans with less than 20% down payment.
ARM
Adjustable Rate Mortgage — fixed initial rate that adjusts periodically based on a market index.
Investment Strategy

Investment Strategy

ARV
After Repair Value — what the property is worth post-rehab.
Cap Rate
Annual NOI divided by property value — yield without leverage.
Cash-on-Cash Return
Annual cash flow divided by cash invested — leveraged yield.
NOI
Net Operating Income — what the property earns after operating costs.
IRR
Annualized total return on investment including sale.
Cash-Out Refinance
Replace your mortgage with a larger one — pocket the equity.
Rate-and-Term Refinance
Refinance to optimize rate or term — no cash extracted.
Refinance
Replace your existing mortgage with a new one on better terms.
Financial Terms

Financial Terms

PITIA
All-in monthly housing cost: principal, interest, taxes, insurance, association.
DTI
Percentage of monthly income going to debt payments.
Reserves
Liquid funds the lender requires after closing.
Origination Fee
One-time fee for processing the loan.
Discount Points
Optional fee at closing to buy down your rate.
Closing Costs
Fees and expenses paid at the closing of a transaction.
Title Insurance
One-time policy protecting against title defects.
Principal
The portion of your payment that reduces what you owe.
Down Payment
Cash paid upfront — determines your LTV and loan size.
Equity
What you own outright — value minus debt secured against the property.
Amortization
The schedule that splits payments between principal and interest.
Escrow
Neutral third party at closing — and the ongoing tax/insurance reserve.
APR
Annual Percentage Rate — total annual cost of a loan including interest rate plus fees.
FICO Score
Credit score from 300-850 that lenders use to evaluate creditworthiness on mortgage applications.
Mortgage Points
Upfront fees paid at closing — discount points lower your rate; origination points compensate the lender.
Industry Terms

Industry Terms

Foreclosure
Legal process by which a lender takes possession of a property after borrower default.
Cost Segregation
Accelerate depreciation by reclassifying property components into shorter recovery periods.
Depreciation
Deduct the cost of an income property over time — a core real estate tax advantage.
K-1
The IRS form reporting your share of income from a partnership or LLC.
REI
Real Estate Investing — the umbrella term for building wealth through property.
SFR
Single-Family Residence — the most common property type for investors.
Multi-family
Residential property with 2 or more units.
Owner-Occupied
Property where the owner lives as primary residence.
